Grammatical Explanation

The most common way of expressing similarity or equivalence in modern Iranian Persian is the Arabic loan work مثل, which is used in the same manner as the etymologically Persian монанд / مانند: both words are common in both dialects, with mānand more frequent in Tajik. For instance, "a lion is not like him": شیر مثلِ / مانندِ او نباشد.

Central Asian Turkic languages usually express similarity with a postfix: -dek / دەك. For instance, "fight like a lion": sherdek kurashing.

Central Asian Persian historical texts tend to favor a construction more similar in function to -dek than to mānand-i or mis̱l-i, in that it is a postfix. For instance, "fight like a lion": شیر‌برین جنگ کنید.

Students more familiar with classical Persian should take care not to mistake this homophone for barīn ('sublime,' 'lofty') or the contraction bar + ēn ("at / on this").
