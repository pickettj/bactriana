Grammatical Explanation

Central Asian Persian has a special verbal mode to express likelihood, which it shares with Central Asian Turkic langauges such as Uzbek and Uyghur. This construction is common both in spoken Tajik and in Central Asian Persian chancery documents, but less common in Central Asian Persian manuscripts.

The basic form is: the continuous particle (mē) + continuous participle (e.g., karda) + gī + ast. For instance: او می‌رفته‌گی‌است, "he is likely to go." In colloquial Tajik, this would be compounded as: мерафтагист.

Although this mode does not have a direct parallel in modern Iranian Persian, the logic closely matches several construcitons in Central Asian Turkic languages. For instance, in Uzbek: u boradigan ekan, "he is likely to go."

  • JP: check Uyghur manuals: there may be another way to express a similar idea

In Iranian Persian, this idea would be expressed with an additional clause (just like in English): احتمال دارد که او برود, "it is likely that he will go".

Examples

bukh: در میانه یگان هفته داخل ولایت چهارجوی میشده گی است

uzb: bir hafta orasida Chorjuy viloyatida bo'ladigan ekan

fars: احتمال دارد که ضرف یک هفته داخل ولایت چهارجوی بشود

eng: He is likely to be in Chorjuy province within a week

Comments:
