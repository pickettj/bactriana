This petition from the most lowliest of supplicants — the smallest mote of the humblest of servants, seeker of [your] satisfaction — Mulla Muhammad Mahdi Ra'is; [being submitted] to the loftiest heights of those granted audience to the royal court, the pivot of justice; with a hundred thousand deficiencies; with the intention and hope of being received with generosity; [the contents of the petition is] as follows:

Oh Servant-Succoring Lord!

13 دعاگویشان از دعوی مذکورش بصواب دید آقسقالان ببدل هزار تنگه
14 صاف شده بذات عالیمولایم دعا نمودند

A woman named Katta-Oy, from the locale of Khatcha in the district of Khabarabad, petitioned My Most Resplendant Sovereign.

She claimed blood money on behalf of her daughter, named Qara-Oy, against Abd al-Khaliq of Samjan district, writing her claim on the back of My Sovereign's legal protocol (maḥżar).

In response, My Sovereign graciously appointed Ghiyās̱ al-Dīn as his deputy (maḥram), who came [to oversee the legal proceedings].

The plaintiff [Katta-Oy] agreed to a settlement of one thousand Tanga in compensation.

  • agreed to a settlement: بصواب‌دیدِ آقسقالان... صاف شده

This solution was prescribed during the course of the legal proceedings presided over by Qazi Isma'il Khwaja, who consulted the judgement of the village elders.

[As the matter was brought to conclusion,] all present gave prayers for the benefit of the Sovereign.

I humbly present my servitude and plead ignorance, as this humble servant, a mere pastor seeking grace, has reported that which he saw and heard to the Sovereign.

May the divine sun of the dynasty, and the shadow of beneficence, grace the heads of all its servants, especially the head of this ignorant servant.

May [the dynasty] shine forever.

Amen.

Forgive me for my miserable mistakes.
