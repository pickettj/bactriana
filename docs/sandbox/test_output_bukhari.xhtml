Grammatical Explanation

The "participle-ization" of Central Asian Persian stands among the features that most distinguishes it from classical and modern Iranian Persian. It is also -- not coincidentally -- the grammatical logic that makes it most similar to Turkic languages.

Modern Iranian Persian uses relative particles, much like English: که works much like "that" or "which." [[example sentence]]

Turkic languages strongly favor participles to express the same ideas for which Iranian Persian (or English) would favor a relative particle. [[examples]]

Examples

بمکتوب از وجه محافظت کردن بعضی نشانگاه اسباب قدیمی میبرامده گی توابعات بخارای شریف اعلام نموده اند

Maktubda Buxoroyi Sharifga tobe' hududlardan chiqadigan qadimi arxeologik ashyolar bor ba'zi joylarni muhofazat qilishni ma'lum qilgan.
