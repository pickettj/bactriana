Grammatical Explanation

The "participle-ization" of Central Asian Persian stands among the features that most distinguishes it from classical and modern Iranian Persian. It is also -- not coincidentally -- the grammatical logic that makes it most similar to Turkic languages.

Modern Iranian Persian uses relative particles, much like English: که works much like "that" or "which." For instance: "The student read the book, which his teacher had given to him" (донишҷӯ китобе хонд ки устодаш онро ба ӯ додаастدانشجو کتابی خواند که استادش آن را به او داده بود)

The same idea would be expressed in Central Asian Persian as follows: شاگرد کتابی استاذش داده گی را خواند: "The student read the given-to-him-by-his-teacher book." Grammatically, استاذش داده گی is functioning as an adjective modifying کتابی, just like a participle in English.

This grammatical logic is foreign to modern Iranian Persian, but standard to Central Asian Turkic languages. For instance, the same sentence would be rendered as talaba o‘qituvchisi bergan kitobni o‘qib chiqdi / ئوقۇغۇچى ئوقۇتقۇچى بەرگەن كىتابنى ئوقۇپ چىقتى .

Examples

بمکتوب از وجه محافظت کردن بعضی نشانگاه اسباب قدیمئ میبرامده گی توابعات بخارای شریف اعلام نموده اند

Maktubda Buxoroyi Sharifga tobe' hududlardan chiqadigan qadimi arxeologik ashyolar bor ba'zi joylarni muhofazat qilishni ma'lum qilgan.

دستور محافظت از بناهای توابع بخارای شریف را که از آنها اشیاء قدیمی استخراج شده است کتباً اعلام کرده اند

A letter commanded that the archeological ruins, which had been discovered outside of Bukhara, should be protected.

Comments:
  • Note that adjectives (and therefore participles) precede the modified noun in Turkic languages, but come after the noun in Persian. In this example, an izofa construction connects اسبابِ قدیمئ to میبرامده گی
  • The verb برآمدن is the standard verb for 'to leave,' 'to exit' in modern Tajik as well (баромадан), but not used in Iranian Persian.

در قیشلاق سرای حدثه واقع شده در آنجاه استقامت داشته گی رعیه روسیه عبد مۇمن ولد محمد حسین را بقتل رسانیداند

u yerda turadigan rus fuquaro

تابعِ روس، که آنجا استقامت داشت

In the village of Saray an incident occurred: a Russian subject, Abd al-Mumin ibn Muhammad Husayn, who resided there, was murdered.

Comments:
  • Note also the use of the gerund واقع شده ('taking place'): as discussed in chapter [[]], this is style favored in Turkic languages (-ib) to break up parts of the sentence, which would usually be expressed with a conjunction (و) in classical Persian or a sentence break in modern Persian.

عرضه های تاتار ها بتوغریگی مایانرا حکومت بخارا بوقت شهر در آمدن اما دستگیر نموده حبس میکرده اند گفته گی دروغ بوده جماعهً مذکور بعرض فاسد اینطریقه عرض را کرده اند

Tatarlarning da'wosiga ko'ra, haqiqatda Buxoro hukumati shaharga kirishda bizlarni ushlab, qamab qo'ymoqda deganlari yo'lg'on bo'lib mazkur jamoa noto'g'ri arz qilgan.
